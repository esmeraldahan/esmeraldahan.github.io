Save Earth

Venice, one of the world's most iconic and unique cities, is facing an uncertain future due to the increasing threat of rising sea levels. In recent years, the city has suffered devastating flooding events that have caused billions of euros in damage and left much of the city submerged in water.

The frequency and severity of these flooding events are only expected to worsen as climate change accelerates and sea levels continue to rise. In response, the city has launched a multi-billion euro plan to build a series of mobile flood barriers, known as MOSE, to protect the city from future flooding.

However, there are concerns that these barriers may not be enough to save Venice in the long term, and that the city may eventually be forced to abandon its unique location and move to higher ground.

The future of Venice is uncertain, but what is clear is that urgent action is needed to address the global threat of climate change and protect our planet's most vulnerable cities and communities.
